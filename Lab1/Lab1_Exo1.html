Brief History

"Exalted is He who took His Servant by night from al-Masjid al-Haram to al-Masjid al-Aqsa, whose surroundings We have blessed, to show him of Our signs. Indeed, He is the Hearing, the Seeing." (Surah Al-Isra, Verse 1)

Palestine represents the heart of the Islamic world and its precious jewel. It is the land of Al-Isra and Mi'raj, the night journey of Prophet Muhammad ï·º, and the first Qibla and third holiest site in Islam. Palestine embraces Al-Aqsa Mosque, which Allah has blessed its surroundings, and it is one of the three mosques to which journey is recommended.

The Blessed Land witnessed the journeys of prophets and messengers, from Abraham (Ibrahim) to Jesus (Isa), peace be upon them, up to the final Prophet Muhammad ï·º. It is a holy land connected to the history of Islam from its beginning and was the first Qibla of Muslims before the direction was changed to the Kaaba.

Throughout history, Palestine has remained a symbol of Islamic resilience and a stronghold of resistance against invaders, defended by Muslims across ages. Today, Al-Aqsa Mosque and Jerusalem remain in the hearts of Muslims worldwide, representing a central cause for the entire Islamic Ummah.
