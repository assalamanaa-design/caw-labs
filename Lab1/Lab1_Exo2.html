Main Content

Since October 7, Gaza has witnessed a devastating humanitarian crisis. Thousands have been killed and injured, including many children and women, and millions have been displaced. Hospitals, schools and other vital infrastructure suffered extensive damage.

Reports (replace with official sources when presenting) mention high numbers of martyrs and wounded, including a tragic toll among children and women. Infrastructure losses include destroyed homes, damaged hospitals and blocked supply lines that worsen shortages of food, water and medical aid.

The humanitarian situation is urgent: many hospitals are overwhelmed, and fuel/electricity shortages hamper care and water supply. (Ref: sehatty.ps / media reports)

The suffering continues as humanitarian aid struggles to enter the area. When an image is inline and floated, text wraps around it. The ongoing blockade has severely limited the flow of essential supplies, leaving millions of Palestinians in desperate need of food, medicine, and basic necessities.

About the Famine Crisis

The famine in Gaza has reached catastrophic levels, with over 90% of the population facing acute food insecurity. Children are particularly vulnerable, with many suffering from severe malnutrition.
